Rather than offering us a detailed description of the artistic beauties of our region, Charles Dickens, on his journey from Genoa to Bologna, with short stops in Piacenza, Parma and Modena, better captures the wonders of the landscape.<br/>
Thus in the journey along the Via Emilia, crossing the Po Valley, he describes the 'plantation', that is the arrangement of rows of trees to protect the vine: a particularity of the rural environment, now canceled by the methods of intensive agriculture.<sup><a href="#1">1</a></sup>
