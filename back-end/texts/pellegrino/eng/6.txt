In Pictures from Italy Dickens tells us that, upon his arrival in Bologna, there was a large influx of "tourists" in the city, who had been prevented from continuing on their way to Florence by a flood. For this reason, Dickens had to stay on the top floor of the hotel:
"In an out-of-the-way room, which I could never find, containing a bed, big enough for a boarding school, which I couldn’t fall asleep in" with only the company of the swallows in the broad eaves over the window.”<sup><a href="#6">6</a></sup>
