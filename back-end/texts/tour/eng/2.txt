The expression "Grand Tour" is present, for the first time, in the volume Voyage of Italy, or a Complete Journey through Italy, written by Richard Lassels and published in 1670.
Initially, the practice was particularly widespread in England and the Calais-Paris section became the main transit route;
The figure of the cosmopolitan intellectual, citizen of the world, was born, who saw and knew directly the natural, cultural and artistic riches, traditions and customs and habits of other countries, thus enriching his own cultural formation.
