Travelling from France to Italy, Dickens makes the sound of the carriage racing with great clatter on a terribly irregular and disjointed pavement:

“Crack, crack, crack, crack. Crack-crack-crack. Crick-crack. Crick-crack. Helo! Hola! Vite! Voleur! Brigand! Hi hi hi! En r-r-r-r-r-route! [...] crick-crack-crick-crack; crick, crick, crick; bump, jolt, crack, bump, crick-crack;...”<sup><a href='#1'>1</a></sup>
