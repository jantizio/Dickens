And again in the chiostro terzo, Dickens will have admired the il Caprara monument (1817 circa), a marble masterpiece of the period and also the first marble group to be made for the Certosa. Made by the sculptor Giacomo De Maria, who had been a student of Antonio Canova, and created entirely in Carrara marble, it is still today one of the most important neoclassical monuments of the Certosa.
