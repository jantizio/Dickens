Then, turning around he will have set off along the wide avenue lined with centuries old yew trees and will have left the Certosa coming out of the new nineteenth century Piangoloni entrance, which used to be the entrance of the deceased, designed by architect Ercole Gasparini, and completed in 1809 with the imposing terracotta statues made by Giovanni Putti (1771-1847), on the large pillars of the entrance. The Bolognese Giovanni Putti was, with Giacomo de Maria, the greatest sculptor in Napoleonic Bologna and of the Restoration period.
